EV vs Gas: The Real Cost of Ownership

By EV Time Team · 5 min read

Everyone talks about sticker price. But that's the wrong number to look at. The real question is: what does this car cost me over 5 years? That's where EVs start to pull away.

The Sticker Price Myth

Yes, EVs often cost more upfront. A new Tesla Model 3 starts around $39,000. A comparable Honda Civic is around $26,000. That's a $13,000 gap. Looks bad for the EV, right?

Not so fast. That gap starts shrinking the moment you drive off the lot.

Fuel Costs: It's Not Even Close

The average American drives about 13,500 miles per year. Here's what that costs in fuel:

CategoryEVGas Car
Annual fuel/energy cost$620$2,100
5-year fuel cost$3,100$10,500
Savings over 5 years$7,400 saved with EV

That's based on the national average of $0.16/kWh for electricity and $3.50/gallon for gas. If you charge at home during off-peak hours, it's even cheaper.

Maintenance: The Hidden Win

Gas engines have hundreds of moving parts. Oil changes, transmission fluid, timing belts, spark plugs, exhaust systems. EVs have almost none of that.

  • Oil changes: $0 (EVs don't have engine oil)
  • Brake pads: Last 2-3x longer thanks to regenerative braking
  • Transmission: EVs don't have one
  • Engine repairs: No engine, no repairs

Average maintenance cost over 5 years: $1,600 for an EV vs $4,600 for a gas car. That's another $3,000 saved.

Tax Credits and Incentives

Depending on the model and your state, you could get $3,750 to $7,500 in federal tax credits, plus state incentives. Some states also offer reduced registration fees, HOV lane access, and free charging programs.

Bottom line: When you factor in fuel savings, maintenance, and tax credits, most EVs cost less than comparable gas cars over a 5-year ownership period — even with the higher sticker price.

The 5-Year Total

CategoryEV (Model 3)Gas (Civic)
Purchase price$39,000$26,000
Tax credit-$7,500$0
5-year fuel$3,100$10,500
5-year maintenance$1,600$4,600
Insurance (5 yr avg)$8,500$7,800
5-Year Total$44,700$48,900

The EV wins by $4,200 over 5 years. And that's before factoring in the higher resale value that most EVs hold.

What About Depreciation?

This is where it gets interesting. Teslas in particular hold their value exceptionally well. A 3-year-old Model 3 retains about 70-75% of its value. Most gas sedans retain 55-60%.

If you plan to sell after 3-5 years, the EV likely gives you thousands more back at resale.

The Verdict

The sticker price is a distraction. When you zoom out and look at total cost of ownership, EVs are already cheaper for most drivers. And the gap is only widening as battery costs drop and gas stays unpredictable.

The question isn't whether you can afford an EV. It's whether you can afford not to consider one.
