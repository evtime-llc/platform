Charging at Home vs Public Charging

By EV Time Team · 4 min read

One of the first questions every new EV owner asks: where do I charge this thing? The answer is simpler than you think — and cheaper than you expect.

The Three Levels of Charging

All EV charging falls into three categories. Here's the quick breakdown:

LevelPowerSpeedWhere
Level 1120V (standard outlet)3-5 miles/hourAny wall outlet
Level 2240V (dryer outlet)25-35 miles/hourHome install or public stations
DC Fast (Level 3)400-900V150-200+ miles in 20 minTesla Supercharger, Electrify America, etc.

Home Charging: The Best Deal

A large share of EV charging happens at home. It's the cheapest and most convenient option by far.

Level 1 — The "Just Plug It In" Option

Most EVs come with a cable that plugs into a standard 120V outlet. No installation needed. You get about 3-5 miles of range per hour of charging.

  • Best for: Short commutes under 40 miles/day, plug-in hybrids
  • Cost: $0 — you already have the outlet
  • Reality check: For most pure EVs, this is too slow for daily use unless you drive very little

Level 2 — The Sweet Spot

A 240V outlet (same as your dryer or oven) with a dedicated charging unit. This is what most EV owners install at home.

  • Speed: 25-35 miles of range per hour. A full charge overnight, every night.
  • Installation cost: $500-$1,500 for the electrician + $300-$600 for the wall unit
  • Monthly cost: About $40-$60/month for average driving (13,500 miles/year)

Pro tip: Charge during off-peak hours (usually 9pm-6am). Many utilities offer EV-specific rate plans that cut your cost to $0.08-$0.10/kWh — roughly $30/month for most drivers.

Public Charging: When You Need It

Public charging is for road trips, errands when you're low, or if you don't have home charging available.

Level 2 Public Stations

Found at malls, grocery stores, workplaces, and parking garages. Many are free. Paid ones typically cost $1-$3/hour.

  • Speed: Same as home Level 2, about 25 miles/hour
  • Best for: Topping up while you shop or work
  • Networks: ChargePoint, Blink, EVgo, and many free ones on the PlugShare app

DC Fast Charging

The gas station equivalent. Pull in, plug in, get 150-200 miles of range in 20-30 minutes.

  • Cost: $0.30-$0.50/kWh, or roughly $12-$20 for a typical session
  • Networks: Tesla Supercharger (now open to most EVs), Electrify America, EVgo
  • Best for: Road trips and long-distance travel

The Cost Comparison

These are rough estimates and can vary by utility rates, charger pricing, and vehicle efficiency.

Even DC fast charging — the most expensive EV charging option — is roughly the same cost as gas. Home charging is 3-4x cheaper.

Do You Need a Home Charger?

If you own or rent a place with a garage or dedicated parking spot — yes, absolutely. It's the single best upgrade for EV ownership. You wake up every morning with a full "tank" and never visit a gas station again.

If you live in an apartment without dedicated parking, you can still make it work with workplace charging and weekly public charging stops. It's less convenient but very doable.

Bottom line: Home charging is the cheapest, most convenient way to fuel any vehicle — gas or electric. A Level 2 setup can pay for itself within the first year through fuel savings alone.
